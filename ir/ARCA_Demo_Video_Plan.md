# ARCA — 60-Second Launch Film
### Production plan · *"The Day It Stopped Being Loud"*

> A booth-ready, one-minute film you can build **without shooting any live action** —
> real product capture carries every proof, Higgsfield supplies only atmosphere.
> Aesthetic = **Omi** intimacy → **heyclicky** tactility → **Apple** reverence.

---

## 1. The idea in one breath

A founder is drowning in a storm of incoming pings. The copper companion quietly wakes,
**absorbs the noise**, and at the one moment that needs a human, asks **one thing** — *1·2·3,
with a recommendation*. One tap. *"✓ Sent. Recorded."* The loop closes, the storm is gone,
and the day is handed back.

- **Spine emotion:** overwhelm → companionship → reclaimed calm (deliberately *not* loneliness/surveillance — that's the trap Friend/Omi fell into).
- **Tagline (close):** *"Less managing work. More meaningful work."*
- **Logo line:** *"ARCA closes the loop."* / sub: *"It remembers everything, acts on what it can, and asks you only when it matters."*
- **No voiceover.** Kinetic cream-on-near-black type + an ambient pad that builds to **one drop** on the loop-close. AI VO is the #1 "cheap" tell.

**The three-gaze structure** (the reason it feels like Apple × heyclicky × Omi):

| Act | Seconds | Gaze | Looks at… | Rhythm | Job |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — The noise** | 0–15s | **Omi** | the *life* around it | ~75 BPM, flowing | Make you *feel* the overwhelm |
| **2 — The routing** | 15–40s | **heyclicky** | the *hands/UI* touching it | ~118 BPM, percussive, cut-on-click | Prove it actually works |
| **3 — The one decision** | 40–60s | **Apple** | the *object* (reverence) | drop to silence → full drop | Land the money shot + payoff |

## 2. The golden rule: what's real vs. what's Higgsfield

This is the whole strategy. **Real product capture proves; AI only atmospheres.** If an AI shot ever has to *prove the product works, it's misused* — viewers sense the hero shot isn't real and discount the entire demo.

| Layer | Source | Used for |
|---|---|---|
| **PROOF** (real) | Screen recordings + 1 device photo | Every UI beat, the OLED face, the money-shot card. Carries all credibility. |
| **ATMOSPHERE** (Higgsfield) | Soul stills → image-to-video | The message-storm, the copper core in a void, the device constellation, the hero float-in. **Never** humans' faces, hands on the device, or legible text. |

> You said you can't shoot **live-action video**. Good news: **screen recording isn't shooting** — every UI surface below is capturable today. The *only* camera-style footage (people, hands, device-in-a-room) is exactly what Higgsfield generates. Clean split.

## 4. The Higgsfield build (the ~6 AI shots + how to make them)

**Workflow (do this exactly):**
1. **Soul** (still model) → render each AI scene as a photoreal still first. Use **one "Soul ID"** across all stills so the void, copper grade, and core silhouette stay consistent shot-to-shot.
2. **SOUL Inpaint** → fix any geometry/edges/screen content *in the still*, before motion. Text and fine detail are stable in stills, unstable in video.
3. **Image-to-video** → feed each locked still in, attach **ONE camera preset per clip** (don't stack), generate ~5s. Animate from a still — never text-to-video — so shapes hold.
4. **Start-frame → End-frame** → use for the two "controlled" beats: the hero reveal (dark void → lit Core) and the logo morph.
5. Stitch 8–12 clips in your editor. **All legible text/logos = post overlays**, never rendered by the video engine.

**Shot list + prompts** (copper palette baked into every prompt: near-black `#0A0604`, copper `#DC5000`/`#FF6A1A`, amber `#E8A04B`):

| Shot | Preset | Soul-still prompt (then animate) |
|---|---|---|
| **A1** Storm slam (0–3s) | **FPV Drone** | *"first-person plunge through a dark void filled with hundreds of glowing glassy message-bubble shards in copper, Slack-purple, kakao-yellow and blue, swirling like a blizzard, near-black #0A0604, volumetric haze, cinematic, no text"* |

**AI safety rules (this is what separates premium from slop):**
- ❌ No AI faces, no hands touching the device, no AI-rendered UI, no AI-rendered legible text/logos. These are the exact failure modes that scream "AI."
- ✅ AI only ever renders **abstract atmosphere** — shards, light, the copper core/object in a void, the constellation. All of those are AI-safe.
- Each Higgsfield generation is ~5–15s; budget **re-rolls** for silhouette/shard-density drift; keep big camera moves short and slow.
- Force the copper palette in *every* prompt **and** push it again in the final grade so AI and real footage match.

## 6. Sound design & music (design this FIRST)

The clicky reference's whole secret: **sound leads, picture follows.** Cut to the audio, not the other way around.

- **Lay the music bed first.** One track with a clear build + a single drop. Map it: minimal under the hook/setup → rising layers + riser through the routing → **deliberate ~0.7s near-silence** at 32–40s → **full drop at 40s** = the money shot.
- **The audio logo:** design **one signature "loop-closed" chime** — a clean, slightly reverberant bell/synth ping. It lands in the silence on the tap. Reuse it across every cutdown; it becomes the ARCA sound.
- **UI foley:** soft mechanical clicks on cursor clicks, low whooshes on transitions/zoom-punches, light "tick/data" texture under fast scrubbing, slider detents. Keep SFX −12 to −18 dB under music; duck music when SFX hits.
- **Cut Act 2 ON the clicks**, not on the musical beat — that's what makes routing feel addictively tactile.
- **Text-only**, no VO. (If you ever want a human line, one real read on the promise only.)

## 7. Finishing — making mixed sources read as ONE film

- **One grade/LUT** across every clip (real + AI) so blacks, contrast, white balance match.
- **One film-grain + subtle bloom layer over the entire timeline** — shared grain is the single strongest "this is one film" cue.
- **Device mocks:** never show raw full-bleed screenshots. Float the recordings inside a clean Mac/iPhone/browser-chrome mock on a copper-gradient ground, soft contact shadow, 1px inner highlight, 3–5% parallax drift so static UI feels alive.
- **Transition vocabulary — pick 2–3 and reuse:** (1) whip-pan + whoosh, (2) light-flash on the beat, (3) match-cut/shape-morph (esp. the circle→wordmark at the end). Variety reads amateur; restraint reads premium.
- **Never hard-cut from an AI shot straight to real UI** — bridge with an abstract AI texture or a graphic transition, welded by a shared whoosh.
- **Typography:** one tight font pairing (one bold display + one clean body), copper as the keyword/accent color, cream for body, one idea per beat, fast eases (word-by-word fade / mask wipe / slight upward drift).
- **Aspect strategy:** master **4K 16:9** with all key action/text in a **center "protect zone"** (~1080px wide). For 9:16 / 1:1 booth + social cutdowns, **reframe** each shot (don't blind-crop) and trim to hook + money shot + CTA. Bake captions for silent autoplay.

1. **Lock ONE money-shot copy string.** The pitch deck says *"민수 / watermarked v0.4 / confidence 0.84"*; the shipped macOS `fixture.json` uses *"Minsoo / ARCA business plan / Zero1ne."* Pick one and make them match — either **edit `fixture.json`** so the screen recording *is* the real thing, or stage a clean post overlay. **Recommended:** `"✓ Sent watermarked v0.4 to Minsoo. Recorded."` (romanized reads internationally; use `민수` if it's a Korean-only crowd). **Don't mix the two spellings across beats.**
2. **No device photo exists yet** — `hardware/` only has `arca_render.png` (a grey parts render). The OLED-comp beats (3, 5, 7, 9) have no plate until you shoot one. **Options, best first:** (a) take one still photo of the real Core on a copper-lit surface; (b) if no photo is possible, generate a photoreal copper Core in **Soul** (seed it from the render + STL silhouette) and animate it; (c) fall back to the existing vector blob companion as a stylized hero.
3. **The macOS app has no "happy/excited" face** — only `idle / listening / thinking / done`. The loop-closed *excited* face must come from the **hardware OLED** (`arca_excited_monster.qgif`) or the web/deck vector face. Don't promise a Mac-app excited face.
4. **Use the real route labels** on screen: *Ignore · Remember · Digest · Draft Reply · Notify Now · Auto Execute* (+ Decide). Inventing "Ask"/"Act" as route chips undercuts the "every proof frame is real" promise.
5. **AI continuity:** one shared Soul ID + identical dark-void background prompt for every AI clip, then the unified grade/grain/bloom. Budget re-rolls.
6. **Runtime discipline:** keep the constellation graft ≤5s and the routing montage tight, or the pre-tap silence (the film's best move) gets compressed and the payoff loses its punch. Target ~58s.

1. **Lock the money-shot copy** (gotcha #1) and update `fixture.json` if going that route.
