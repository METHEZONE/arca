## The whole idea in one breath
ARCA is your second brain that talks back. So in this film **it does the talking** — quietly, a little shyly — and *proves* itself by doing real work in front of you: a morning pre-brief, capturing + remembering a conversation, the signature **ask 1·2·3** decision, and being the same companion across every device. The charm carries it; the product does the proving.

**Why it's different from the pitch you rejected:** that one *argued a thesis* (third-person, abstract, kinetic-type sermon). This one *befriends you* (first-person, concrete, a voice with a soul). The loop-close isn't a slogan here — it's an emotion the creature feels out loud: *"(i kind of love when that works.)"*

## Voice & look (the two decisions that make or break it)
- **Voice = ONE warm, real, slightly-shy human read.** Recorded on a phone in a quiet room — *not* studio-polished, *not* TTS, *not* a robot/character voice. heyclicky's entire charm is a real person giving a drawn character a soul: the tiny breaths, the pause before the bashful line, the proud little exhale. **The face does the character work; the voice does the soul work.** (TTS = the instant "cheap AI" tell. Text-only = forfeits your biggest differentiator and drifts back toward the rejected pitch.)
- **Captions = ARCA's "typed thoughts."** Kinetic type in ARCA's own UI font, cream `#FFEDD7` on near-black `#0A0604`, typed out under the read. Doubles as silent-autoplay safety for a launch tweet, and reinforces "a second brain that types its thoughts."
- **Palette / feel:** warm near-black, copper `#DC5000`/`#FF6A1A`, amber. Intimate, handmade, one-breath-per-beat pacing. Lowercase everywhere.

## ⚠️ Gotchas (verified against the repo — fix before editing)
1. **macOS face states are limited.** The app's `FaceState` enum is only `idle / listening / thinking / done / error / approval` — **no excited / blush / recording / uploading**. Every excited-wiggle, blush, recording-pulse, uploading-cloud beat must be the **real `.qgif` composited as a hybrid inset/cut-in**, never faked inside the Mac app. (All three takes got this right — protect it.)
2. **Use the real Ask-card copy.** Match `fixture.json` (*"Reply to Minsoo — business plan," "Investor requested the latest plan"*) or build a clean overlay in the app's visual language. Don't ship invented labels.
3. **No real device photo exists** (only a grey parts render). The physical Core appears **only** as a Higgsfield render-in-void with the real OLED GIF composited on its screen — this cut deliberately uses the **constellation/void** treatment so you can finish **with zero product shoot**.
4. **Diarization is "rough" (energy/spectral split, self-labeled, not pyannote-grade).** It reads as real at speed — but **don't zoom-hold on transcript accuracy** or make precise-diarization claims in captions.
5. **The 7-routes scene is on the Korean scrollytelling page** — for an English launch-tweet audience it reads as a foreign marketing site mid-demo, so this cut **swaps it for the pre-brief card** (a real Mac surface) to keep every beat feeling like *"a thing arca did,"* not a site tour.
6. **Don't front-load too much void-creature.** Get to the **first real Mac screen by ~0:08–0:10**; keep the opening creature beats to single slow blinks, not elaborate camera moves, or the open reads like a generic intro animation.
