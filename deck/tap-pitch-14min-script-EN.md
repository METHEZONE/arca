# ARCA — TAP4001 14-Minute Pitch (Pitch 1 + 2 Combined)
### English narration script — TTS-ready for ElevenLabs "Min Voice 1"

> **Format:** Recorded voiceover over advancing PPT slides (no live presenter).
> **Workflow:** Generate each slide's narration block in Min Voice 1 → drop the audio under its slide → advance on cue.
> **Voice direction (ElevenLabs):** Stability ~50, Similarity ~75, Style low-mid. Warm, confident, unhurried — Steve Jobs keynote, not a sales rep. Let the *[beat]* lines breathe (insert ~0.8–1.2s silence there).
> **TTS hygiene:** numbers/symbols spelled out; em-dashes (—) and ellipses (…) are pacing cues — keep them.
> **Brand lock:** worldview = **THE ZONE** · companion = **ARCA** (arc + archive + ark) · verb = **arca it** (complete delegation). Slogan: *"Don't carry it. Just arca it."* Never describe ARCA as a noun — always the verb / the companion.
> **Core mechanic:** ARCA has **two modes** — (1) when you're in the zone it stands guard and handles requests silently, never interrupting; (2) when you surface, it lines everything up and you triage — *arca it* or decide. You're never asked to choose mid-flow.
> **Target:** ~14:00. Spoken words ≈ 1,700; the Jobs-style beats + demo screen-time carry it to ~14–15 min.

## SLIDE 1 — Cold Open · "The best ninety minutes of my week" (≈1:45)

>
> And just like that — the room comes back. So I stop. I dig through folders. I find the right version, I send it. I try to climb back in… but the door's already closed.
>
> Five minutes later — another one. *"Send me the quote and we'll wire the payment today."* And that one I can't ignore. No quote… no money. So I open a blank document. And I start building it. From scratch. Again.

> By the time I look up — the ninety minutes are gone. And so is everything I almost figured out.
>
> *[beat]*
>
> And that night I remember thinking — none of that was hard. Any of it could've been done by someone else. I just… didn't have anyone I trusted to do it right.
>

## SLIDE 9 — How it works · TWO MODES (≈1:50) ⭐ core
*Visual: a single split — "IN THE ZONE" (a shield) / "OUT OF THE ZONE" (a clean triage queue).*

> So how does it actually work? Here's the part that makes it different. ARCA has two modes — because *you* have two modes.
>
> *[beat]*
>
> Mode one — you're in the zone. And when you're in the zone, ARCA stands guard. The requests still come. The client still needs that quote. But it doesn't reach you. ARCA catches it, understands it, and handles everything it already can — silently. Your phone stays dark. Your flow stays whole. You are *never* asked "should I arca this?" — because you are never interrupted in the first place. That… is the entire point.
>
> *[beat]*
>
> Mode two — you come up for air. The moment you step out of deep work, ARCA is waiting — with everything that arrived while you were gone, lined up and ready. And now you simply triage. This one? Arca it — gone. ARCA writes the quote, attaches the document, sends it, closes the loop. This one needs your call? Two options, a recommendation, one tap. A minute later, you're clear.
>
> And underneath all of it, quietly — it remembers who asked for what, and it shows you where your hours actually went. So next week… there's a little less to guard against.

## Production notes
- **Per-slide recording:** generate each block separately → place under its slide → add a 0.3–0.5s silence pad before each block so transitions don't clip the first word.
- **The *[beat]* lines:** insert ~0.8–1.2s of real silence. These pauses are doing the Steve-Jobs work — don't let the voice rush them.
- **Bookend:** Slides 1 and 14 must use the **same notification chime**. That sound is the payoff — trap on Slide 1, freedom on Slide 14.
- **Demo (Slide 10):** record the screen capture first, then time narration to it. Leave near-silence right before "I tap… arca it" so the on-screen tap lands in sync.
- **If it runs short of 14:00:** lengthen on-slide animation holds (esp. after Slides 1, 3, 9, 10, 14). Never speed up the voice.
- **One-take alt:** concatenate all 14 blocks in order; the em-dashes/ellipses/beats carry the pacing.
